The RED ONE was designed as a **modular camera system**.

<img src="{{ '/assets/images/modular-design.png' | relative_url }}"
     alt="RED ONE Modular Design"
     class="section-heading-img" />

The base body ships with a PL mount
and a CF Module, but virtually everything else (storage, power, monitoring, support accessories,
and lenses) is an optional add-on. This page catalogs all known accessories from the RED ONE MX era.

> *"Shoot lightweight EFP style (body around 10 lbs., in rugged aluminum alloy) or load it up
> to shoot a feature film. Options include RED-RAIL™ mounting accessories that 'grow' the camera
> body. Configure for tripod, crane or long lens applications."*
> - RED Digital Cinema, 2010

## Digital Storage

### CF Module (CompactFlash)

The CF Module adds an in-camera CompactFlash card slot and is included with every new RED ONE
purchase (+$500 included in camera price). CF cards are also used for firmware upgrades.

- 8GB CF card ≈ 400 ft film reel equivalent (~4–5 minutes at 4K REDCODE 28)
- 16GB CF card ≈ double the 8GB capacity
- Maximum record resolution without speed-verified CF: **2K**

| Item | Price (2011) | Price (2026 avg) |
|---|---|---|
| RED CF MODULE | $500 | ~$45 |
| RED CF CARD 16GB | $300 | ~$5 |
| RED STATION BASE | $250 | ~$50 |
| RED STATION COMPACT FLASH | $250 | ~$50 |

### RED DRIVE™ (RAID)

External RAID-0 magazine housing two 2.5" 160GB laptop drives = **320GB total** capacity.
Approximately **2–3 hours of 4K REDCODE™ RAW** footage. Mounts in RED RAIL CRADLE or directly
to camera body. Connects via Drive Connector LEMO.

> *"Storing two to three hours of 4K REDCODE™ RAW footage on a drive smaller than an ASC manual
> is downright unbelievable."*

| Item | Price (2011) | Price (2026 avg) |
|---|---|---|
| DRIVE CABLE (18") | $200 | ~$15 |
| DRIVE CABLE (6') | $250 | ~$20 |

### RED-RAM™

Same form factor as RED DRIVE, but using two 2.5" 64GB SSDs in RAID-0 = **128GB total**.
Approximately **30–40 minutes of 4K REDCODE™ RAW**.

### REDMAG SSD

Solid-state storage module for the RED ONE MX. Connects via the 26-pin iVDR connector
on the camera body.

| Size | Interface | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 128GB | iVDR 26-pin | SATA II internal; SiI3512 controller |
| 256GB | iVDR 26-pin | Identified drive: Toshiba HG3 series THNSNC256GBSJ |

| Item | Price (2011) | Price (2026 avg) |
|---|---|---|
| RED STATION REDMAG 1.8" | $250 | ~$75 |
| RED STATION REDMAG 2.5" | $250 | ~$75 |

> **Repair note:** The camera validates the drive model string at boot against a hardcoded
> approved list. Replacement drives must match an approved model string or the firmware
> must be patched. See the [Hardware Reference](/hardware) and the
> [r1mx SSD research](https://github.com/simook/r1mx/blob/main/ssd_drive/README.md) for details.

## Power System

### RED BRICK™ Battery

140Wh V-Mount battery designed specifically for the RED ONE. Approximately 2 hours
charge time. Mounts via RED V-MOUNT PLATE or RED BATTERY PLATE.

### RED CHARGER

Sequential V-Mount charger with 3 indicator lights per port. Can charge while connected
to camera; automatically prioritises camera power. Features a small fold-out stand.

## RED-RAIL™ System

The RED-RAIL™ system allows the camera to be configured for any shooting style by
redistributing weight along a central rail. The system supports shoulder-mount,
tripod, crane, and long-lens configurations.

> *"Using the weight of the Digital Magazine and Battery on the extending rail system, you can
> counterbalance the weight of different lenses and accessories to match the desired balance
> point for optimal shooting. The RED-RAIL™ system takes the shoulder mount to the next level,
> with infinite flexibility."*
